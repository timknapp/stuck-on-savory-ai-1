Smoky BBQ Ribs

Smoky BBQ Ribs are a classic barbecue favorite that are tender, juicy, and full of flavor. The homemade smoky rub and tangy barbecue sauce make these ribs a hit at any cookout or family gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tbsp salt
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, ground cumin, black pepper, and salt to create the dry rub.
  2. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and rub the dry rub mixture evenly onto both sides of the ribs.
  3. Wrap the ribs in aluminum foil and let them marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-low heat. Place the foil-wrapped ribs on the grill, bone side down, and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily pulls away from the bone.
  5. Remove the ribs from the foil and brush both sides with barbecue sauce. Grill the ribs for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the sauce is caramelized and sticky.
  6. Remove the ribs from the grill, let them rest for a few minutes, and then cut into individual ribs. Serve immediately and enjoy!
